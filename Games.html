First Generation

 

The Pokemon series began with the release of Pocket Monster Red and Green for the Game Boy in Japan during 1996. When these became really popular an enhanced version Blue was released and soon reprogrammed as Pokemon Red and Blue for an international release. Soon after a Pokemon yellow released to use the colour Palette of the Gameboy colour. The first generation contained 151 species of Pokemon and introduced the games basic concept of capturing, training, battling, and trading Pokemon with both computers and human players

 

Second Generation

 

The second version of Pokemon began in 1999 with the introduction of the Japanese version of Pokemon Gold and Silver for the Gameboy colour and like the previous generation an enhanced version was released "Pokemon Silver". This new generation introduced 100 new species of Pokemon. New game play features were introduced including day and night system which influences events in the games, full use f the Game colour Palette, improved interface, better balance of Pokemon statistics and two new Pokemon types (dark and steel)

 

Thrid Generation

 

The third version were released in 2002 with Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire for Game Boy Advance including a remake of Pokemon Red and Green called Fire Red and Leaf Green. Soon after the enhanced version Pokemon emerald were released . It introduced 135 new Pokemon adding to a total of 386 species. The new features include "natures" affecting Pokemon stats, 2 on 2 battle, special abilities, Pokemon Contest sub game and the new region Hoenn.

 

Fourth Generation

 

In 2006 the release of Pokemon Diamond and Pearl for the Nintendo DS were released leading to the enhanced version of Pokemon platinum. this generation introduced 107 new species of Pokemon. New features include 3D graphics and sprite, online multiplayer trading and battling via Nintendo WIFI Connection, return of day and night system, Pokemon Super contest. This also lead to the remake version of Gold and Silver (Heart Gold and Soul Silver)

 

Fifth Generation

 

In 2010 Pokemon Black and White were released. The games serve as direct sequels to Black and White, featuring returning characters and several new characters and locations, with the legendary Pokemon Kyurem in its new alternate forms serving as the games' mascot. These two games were launched in Japan on September 18, 2010. This generation introduced a total of 156 Pokemon to the game.
